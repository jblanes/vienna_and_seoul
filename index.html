Vienna — good to know

  • The kids' timing is an assumption. I've placed them arriving Fri Dec 18 (family days 18–20). If they come earlier, the family blocks slide forward — just say the word and the Courtyard goes to four rooms from the right night.
  • Sundays are quiet. Shops and the Donau Zentrum mall close — but Christmas markets stay open, so the two Sundays (arrival & departure) lean on coffee and markets, not shopping.
  • Café Central was under renovation into autumn 2026; it should be reopened for December, but I'll confirm before we commit it.
  • Reserve ahead: Figlmüller, Plachutta, and especially the Spanish Riding School performance.

Seoul — good to know

  • Palace days are fixed by closures: Gyeongbokgung shuts Tuesdays (Dec 22, 29), Deoksugung shuts Mondays (Dec 28). The plan already routes around them.
  • "Palace at night" = Deoksugung. Gyeongbokgung's lit-in-darkness night opening only runs in spring and autumn — in December it's a dusk visit (last entry 16:00). Deoksugung carries the after-dark palace.
  • Two buffer days (29 & 30) are deliberate — you've seen Seoul twice, so there's room to rest, repeat, or absorb a snow-day swap.
  • Food steering: everything here is meat-forward. Skip the obvious seafood (jjamppong, haemul-anything, raw fish); BBQ, samgyetang, bossam/jokbal, chimaek, and beef-bone soups are your safe, warming lane.
  • NYE is intentionally quiet — the early Jan 1 flight wins over the countdown.
